Learning Demonstratives

Demonstratives are words we use to point to specific things or people. They can be pronouns (standing alone) or adjectives (used with a noun). Examples include this, that, these, and those.

Demonstrative Pronouns

Demonstrative pronouns stand alone and replace a noun. For example:

This is my favorite book.

Those are very expensive shoes.

Demonstrative Adjectives

Demonstrative adjectives come before a noun and describe it. For example:

I want this apple, not that one.

Can you pass me those papers?

When to Use Them

  • This and these refer to things that are close to you (in time or space).
  • That and those refer to things that are farther away.

Interactive Tips

Tip: If you're unsure, think about distance. Is it near or far? Singular or plural?
