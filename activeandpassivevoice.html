Learning Active and Passive Voice

In English, sentences can be written in two voices: active and passive. Understanding the difference helps make your writing clear and effective.

Active Voice

In active voice, the subject of the sentence performs the action. It is direct and strong.

Example: The cat chased the mouse.

Explanation: The subject (cat) performs the action (chased).

Passive Voice

In passive voice, the subject receives the action. It is less direct and often wordier.

Example: The mouse was chased by the cat.

Explanation: The subject (mouse) receives the action (was chased).

When to Use Each Voice

  • Use active voice when you want your writing to be clear and engaging.
  • Use passive voice when the focus is on the action or the recipient of the action, not the doer. Example: The painting was admired by everyone.

How to Identify and Change Voice

To identify the voice of a sentence, ask yourself:

  • Is the subject performing the action? (Active voice)
  • Is the subject receiving the action? (Passive voice)

To change from passive to active:

  1. Identify the subject performing the action.
  2. Rewrite the sentence so the subject comes first, followed by the verb and object.

Examples

Passive: The book was written by the author.

Active: The author wrote the book.
