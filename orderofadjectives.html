Learning the Order of Adjectives

When using multiple adjectives to describe a noun, there is a specific order that native speakers typically follow. This order helps sentences sound natural and clear.

The Order of Adjectives

The general order of adjectives is as follows:

  1. Quantity: one, two, several
  2. Opinion: beautiful, delicious, ugly
  3. Size: small, large, tiny
  4. Age: old, young, ancient
  5. Shape: round, square, flat
  6. Color: red, blue, yellow
  7. Origin: American, French, Chinese
  8. Material: wooden, plastic, metal
  9. Purpose: cooking (as in a cooking pot), sleeping (as in a sleeping bag)

Examples

Correct: I bought a beautiful small red wooden chair.

Correct: She has three large old square tables in her dining room.

Incorrect: I bought a wooden red small beautiful chair.

Tips for Remembering

  • Use the acronym OSASCOMP (Opinion, Size, Age, Shape, Color, Origin, Material, Purpose) to remember the order.
  • Practice by describing objects around you using multiple adjectives.

Interactive Tips

Tip: Try rearranging the adjectives in a sentence to see what sounds natural versus awkward. Stick to the order above!
