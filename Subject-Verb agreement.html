Learning Subject-Verb Agreement

Subject-verb agreement means the subject and verb in a sentence must match in number. If the subject is singular, the verb must be singular. If the subject is plural, the verb must be plural.

Basic Rules

Here are some common rules for subject-verb agreement:

  • A singular subject takes a singular verb. Example: She runs every morning.
  • A plural subject takes a plural verb. Example: They play soccer on weekends.
  • When a subject is joined by "and," use a plural verb. Example: My brother and sister are coming.
  • When a subject is joined by "or" or "nor," the verb agrees with the part closest to it. Example: Neither the teacher nor the students are ready.

Examples

Correct: The dog barks loudly.

Correct: The dogs bark loudly.

Correct: Either the manager or the employees attend the meeting.

Common Mistakes

  • Be careful with indefinite pronouns like everyone, someone, and nobody. These are singular. Example: Everyone is happy.
  • Watch out for collective nouns like team or group. These are usually singular. Example: The team wins the game.

Interactive Tips

Tip: When in doubt, find the subject of the sentence and decide if it is singular or plural. Then choose the matching verb.
